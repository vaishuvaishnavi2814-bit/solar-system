SOLAR SYSTEM

ABOUT SOLAR SYSTEM

  • A gravitationally bound system containing the Sun and all objects orbiting it
  • Formed 4.6 billion years ago from a giant cloud of gas and dust (nebula)
  • The Sun contains 99.86% of the Solar System's total mass
  • Located in the Milky Way galaxy, in the Orion Arm
  • Takes about 230 million years to complete one orbit around the galaxy

SUN

  • The Sun is a yellow dwarf star at the center of our solar system, composed mainly of hydrogen and helium.
  • The Sun is the center of the Solar System and contains 99.86% of its total mass, with its gravity holding all planets, moons, and other objects in orbit.
  • It is a star (G-type main-sequence star/yellow dwarf), not a planet, and produces its own light and heat through nuclear fusion in its core.
  • The Sun is massive with a diameter 109 times Earth's, and more than 1 million Earths could fit inside it.
  • It has extreme temperatures with a core at 15 million°C and surface at 5,500°C, made mostly of hydrogen and helium.
  • The Sun is essential for life on Earth as it provides all the light and heat needed, and without its energy, life as we know it could not exist.

MERCURY

  • Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun in the Solar System.
  • It is the smallest planet, only slightly larger than Earth’s Moon.
  • Mercury is a rocky (terrestrial) planet with a solid, cratered surface.
  • Its surface looks similar to the Moon, with many impact craters and rugged plains.
  • Mercury orbits the Sun once every 88 Earth days, the shortest orbital period of all planets.
  • One rotation on its axis takes about 59 Earth days, so a full day–night cycle is about 176 Earth days.
  • Mercury has no atmosphere to speak of, so heat escapes quickly.
  • Daytime temperatures can reach about 430°C, while night temperatures can drop to about ‑180°C.
  • Despite being closest to the Sun, Venus is hotter due to its thick atmosphere and greenhouse effect.
  • Mercury has a large iron core, which produces a weak magnetic field.
  • It has no natural moons and no rings.
  • There is evidence of water ice in permanently shadowed polar craters where sunlight never reaches.

VENUS

  • Venus is the second planet from the Sun.
  • It is the brightest planet in the night sky, after the Moon.
  • Venus is often called Earth’s “twin” or “sister” planet because it is very similar in size, mass, and density.
  • It is the hottest planet in the Solar System, even hotter than Mercury.
  • Surface temperatures are about 460–475°C (around 870–900°F), enough to melt lead.
  • Venus has an extremely thick, toxic atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid.
  • Atmospheric pressure on the surface is about 90–93 times the pressure at sea level on Earth.
  • Venus has retrograde rotation: it spins clockwise (backwards) compared to most planets.
  • One rotation on its axis takes about 243 Earth days, longer than its year.
  • One orbit around the Sun takes about 225 Earth days, so a Venus year is shorter than its day.
  • Venus has no moons and no rings.
  • Its surface is rocky with many volcanoes, plains, and highland regions, but it is hidden by thick clouds.
  • Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty.

EARTH

  • Earth is the third planet from the Sun.
  • It is the only known planet that supports life.
  • About 70% of Earth’s surface is covered by water.
  • Earth has one moon.
  • It takes about 365.25 days to revolve around the Sun.
  • It rotates once in about 24 hours, causing day and night.
  • Earth has four main layers: crust, mantle, outer core, and inner core.
  • Its atmosphere protects life and helps keep the planet warm.
  • Earth’s surface includes continents, oceans, mountains, deserts, and forests.
  • The planet is about 4.5 billion years old.

MARS

  • Mars is a dusty, cold, desert-like planet.
  • It has a very thin atmosphere, made mostly of carbon dioxide.
  • Mars is smaller than Earth and is the second smallest planet in our solar system.
  • It has two moons, called Phobos and Deimos.
  • A day on Mars is about 24 hours and 37 minutes long.
  • A year on Mars lasts about 687 Earth days.
  • Mars has the highest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons.
  • Its surface has mountains, valleys, craters, and polar ice caps.
  • Scientists have found signs that Mars once had liquid water.
  • Mars has been explored by many rovers, including Curiosity and Perseverance.

JUPITER

  • Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun.
  • It is the largest planet in our solar system.
  • It is a gas giant, made mostly of hydrogen and helium.
  • Jupiter is more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined.
  • It has the shortest day of all the planets, taking about 9.9 hours to rotate once.
  • It takes about 11.9 Earth years to orbit the Sun.
  • Jupiter has at least 95 known moons.
  • Its four biggest moons are Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.
  • Jupiter has a famous giant storm called the Great Red Spot.
  • It has faint rings around it.
  • Jupiter is very cold in its upper atmosphere.
  • It has a very strong magnetic field.

SATURN

  • Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun.
  • It is the second-largest planet in the solar system after Jupiter.
  • Saturn is a gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium.
  • It is famous for its beautiful and bright ring system.
  • Saturn has many moons, including Titan, its largest moon.
  • A day on Saturn lasts about 10.7 hours.
  • A year on Saturn lasts about 29.4 Earth years.
  • Saturn is very light for its size and is less dense than water.
  • It has strong winds and a cold atmosphere.
  • Saturn is named after the Roman god Saturn.

URANUS

  • Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun.
  • It is the third-largest planet in our solar system.
  • It is an ice giant, made mostly of hydrogen, helium, water, ammonia, and methane.
  • Uranus has a blue-green color because of methane in its atmosphere.
  • It is very cold and windy.
  • Uranus rotates on its side, with a tilt of about 98 degrees.
  • A day on Uranus lasts about 17 hours.
  • A year on Uranus lasts about 84 Earth years.
  • It has faint rings around it.
  • Uranus has 27 known moons.
  • It was the first planet discovered with a telescope.
  • It was discovered by William Herschel in 1781.

NEPTUNE

  • Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun.
  • It is an ice giant, made mostly of hydrogen, helium, water, ammonia, and methane.
  • Neptune is deep blue because of methane in its atmosphere.
  • It is the fourth-largest planet in the solar system.
  • Neptune has very strong winds and big storms.
  • A day on Neptune is about 16 hours long.
  • A year on Neptune is about 164.8 Earth years.
  • It has faint rings around it.
  • Neptune has 16 known moons.
  • Its largest moon is Triton.
  • Neptune was discovered in 1846.
  • It cannot be seen with the naked eye.
